# ⚖️ The Patient’s Right to Record

### 1. Know Your State Laws
In the United States, recording laws fall into two categories:

* **One-Party Consent:** You can record as long as YOU know it's happening. You aren't legally required to tell the doctor.
* **Two-Party Consent:** Everyone in the room must agree. (Common in CA, FL, IL, MA).

### 🛠 2. AI Recording Tools
Don't just use a "Voice Memo." Use these for searchable transcripts:
* **Otter.ai:** Great for real-time summaries.
* **Heidi Health:** Turns visits into clinical notes.
* **Abridge:** Designed for medical conversations.

### 🗣 3. The "Unhinged" Scripts
**The Collaborative Approach:** "I have major brain fog during flares. I’m going to run this AI scribe so I don't miss any of your instructions. That works for you, right?"

**The Accountability Approach:** "I am recording this session for my personal health records to correlate with my biometric data."

### 🛑 4. If they say "NO"
1. **Ask Why:** "Is there a specific policy against patients recording their own health info?"
2. **The Charting Move:** "Please ensure your refusal to let me record my health summary is noted in today's visit summary."
